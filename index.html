Mission Overview
Primary Mission

Accurate Measurement: Our primary focus is to measure temperature and pressure at various altitudes to study atmospheric conditions and their changes effectively.

Temperature Variations: By accurately measuring temperature, we aim to gain insights into temperature variations in the atmosphere. This will help us understand how these variations influence weather patterns and climate dynamics.

Climate Research: The data collected will contribute to climate research, enabling us to analyze temperature trends, identify temperature inversions, and examine temperature gradients at different altitudes.

Secondary Mission

Gas Detection: We aim to measure specific gases present in the atmosphere using a gas sensor. This will allow us to detect and quantify the presence of gases such as carbon dioxide, methane, and pollutants, providing valuable information for environmental analysis.

Humidity Analysis: To further understand weather patterns and atmospheric stability, we have included a humidity sensor in our CanSat. This sensor will measure and analyze the moisture content in the atmosphere, which influences cloud formation, precipitation, and overall atmospheric conditions.

Visual Documentation: Two mini cameras have been integrated into our payload to document the CanSat's descent and gather additional insights. The captured video footage will provide a unique perspective of the environment and aid in post-mission analysis.

Origami Deploying Solar Array: Inspired by BYU's collaboration with NASA's Jet Propulsion Laboratory, we have implemented an origami deploying solar array. This solar array will generate and measure electricity during the mission, ensuring a sustainable power source.

AI-Powered Analysis: To optimize the analysis of the captured video footage, our system incorporates AI technology. By leveraging AI algorithms, we can identify and track various objects of interest, such as land, trees, bodies of water, or specific landmarks, providing valuable insights into the CanSat's surroundings during its descent.

System Redundancy: To mimic the internal systems used in modern satellites, we have implemented a dual microcontroller module setup into our project. These redundant systems ensure seamless operation, allowing one module to take over in case of any failures, eliminating downtime. Both operate under a Slave-Master approach and all share access to every module, including the radio transmitter.

The Hardware
Teensy 4.1

The Teensy 4.1 microcontroller is an excellent choice for our project due to its impressive processing power and compact size. With a 600 MHz ARM Cortex-M7 processor, it offers superior computational capabilities, enabling advanced data processing and control tasks. The Teensy 4.1 also boasts a wide range of peripheral interfaces, including multiple UART, SPI, and I2C ports, making it easy to connect and communicate with various sensors and modules used. Additionally, it supports high-speed SD card access, ensuring efficient storage of data collected during the mission. Its small form factor and low power consumption make it ideal for the limited space and power constraints set out for the competition

LoRa RMF95W

The LoRa RMF95W is an indispensable component of our CanSat teams toolkit. Its long-range capabilities make it ideal for our telemetry needs. Its special radio modulation, operating either at 868MHz or 915MHz, offers a range of approximately 2 kilometres, which can be extended up to 20 kilometres with directional antennas and settings tweakings. This is pivotal in ensuring reliable data transmission from our CanSat even from higher altitudes. It operates at a power output of up to 100 mW, which is selectable in software, providing us with the flexibility to manage power usage based on specific mission requirements. Furthermore, the RFM95W is compact, with dimensions of 29mm x 25mm x 4mm and weighing only 3.1g, which is an essential attribute considering the stringent weight and size constraints of our CanSatвЂ‹ Project

ESP32-CAM

The ESP32-CAM module is an excellent choice for CanSat, offering a versatile and compact solution for capturing images and transmitting them wirelessly. With its integrated camera and powerful ESP32 microcontroller, it provides a comprehensive platform for capturing visual data during the mission. The ESP32-CAM supports various communication protocols, including Wi-Fi and Bluetooth, allowing seamless connectivity to ground stations or other devices. It also offers ample storage options with its MicroSD card slot, ensuring reliable and convenient storage of high-resolution images. The module operates at low power, making it great for our project with limited energy resources. Its compact form factor enables easy integration into the CanSat structure, making it an ideal choice for capturing and transmitting visual data in real-time during the mission

Neo M9N GPS

The SparkFun NEO-M9N GPS Breakout is a top-quality GPS board with excellent configuration options. It features a 92-channel u-blox M9 engine GNSS receiver, capable of receiving signals from GPS, GLONASS, Galileo, and BeiDou constellations with ~1.5 metre accuracy. This breakout allows concurrent reception of four GNSS, maximising position accuracy even in challenging conditions. With the onboard rechargeable battery, the GPS can achieve a hot lock within seconds. Moreover, it supports I2C communication (Display Data Channel). It also provides real-time position, velocity and time information, enabling us to track the CanSat's location throughout the mission.

Origami

To ensure a sustainable and independent power supply for our CanSat, we have incorporated a solar array into our design. The array consists of 18 cells, 12 (type A) of which are rated at 3V and 22mA, and 6 (type B) of which are rated at 4.2V and 22mA. The dimensions of type A cells are 64mm by 37mm, while type B cells are 84mm by 37mm. These cells are wired in series in groups of 6, with two type A's and one type B in each group. This provides a peak output of 10.2V and 22mA for each group. Finally, the 6 groups are wired in parallel to give a total peak power output of 10.2V and 132mA. The solar array, a crucial element, captures sunlight and transforms it into electricity, powering the CanSat's functions efficiently. This process uses photovoltaic cells to optimise energy capture and ensure constant operational support. Our array will have a total surface area of 470.64cmВІ.

BNO055 IMU

The Adafruit 9-DOF Absolute Orientation IMU Fusion Breakout - BNO055 is an excellent choice for CanSat missions, offering precise and reliable orientation sensing capabilities in a compact package. With its integrated accelerometer, gyroscope, and magnetometer, the BNO055 provides accurate and real-time measurement of the CanSat's position and orientation. This is crucial for tracking the CanSat's altitude, altitude and heading during the mission. The BNO055 features a built-in fusion algorithm, that combines sensor data, to provide reliable and stable orientation outputs, reducing the need for complex sensor fusion programming. Its compact size allows for easy integration into the CanSat structure. With its wide operating voltage range and low power consumption, the BNO055 is a great choice for our requirements

Adafruit 904

As a CanSat team, we heavily rely on the Adafruit 904 for its essential role in our power management. This INA219 high-side DC current sensor breakout board is a vital tool in our toolkit. It monitors and manages our CanSat's power supply, ensuring that the CanSat's electrical systems function optimally throughout the mission. With its ability to handle up to 26V and 3.2A, it is perfectly suited for our power requirements. The IВІC interface allows for straightforward integration with our onboard computer system. Plus, its compact dimensions help us maintain our stringent weight and size limitations. Overall, the Adafruit 904 plays a critical role in our CanSat's reliable and efficient operation.

Our project uses the BME680 which is a digital 4-in-1 sensor with gas, humidity, pressure and temperature measurement based on proven sensing principles.
